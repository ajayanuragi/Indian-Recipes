Naan

What Is Naan?

Naan (pronounced nahn) is an oven-baked, leavened bread that is stuffed and flavored in a variety of ways.
An essential part of South and Central Asian cuisine, naan is easy, affordable, and makes the most of pantry ingredients you already have on hand.
Flour, yeast, milk, and butter make a tender dough that's simply seasoned with garlic and salt, but there is a wide array of naan bread flavors.
You'll find everything from coconut and raisin-stuffed bread to saucy lamb-topped naan in restaurants and home kitchens around the world.

How to Make Naan?

    Dissolve yeast in warm water in a large bowl. Let stand about 10 minutes, until frothy.

    Meanwhile, generously oil a large bowl.

  • Step 4

    Meanwhile, preheat a large grill pan over high heat.

    Brush some melted butter on the preheated grill pan.
